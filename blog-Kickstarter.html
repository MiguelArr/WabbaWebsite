EXPERIENCE



In October of 2023 I ran a KICKSTARTER campaign for my game Sorceress, it was successful, it met its goal and then some more, it was a taxing experience, I learned a lot from it and I'd like to share it.

If you're a game developer doing something somewhat big and somewhat complicated, sooner or later you're going to need money to keep going, that much is obvious, now, as a game developer you have a few different trees you can shake to get that dough, some devs live in a comfortable situation and can just peacefully work while either having constant revenue or enough savings.

Other devs either approach or get approached by a publisher that offers funding and probably other things too to sweeten the deal, this can come with a lot of caveats and I have many things to say about this, but this is not the time.

Which brings me to a third option, getting funded by the people, and the commons way of doing this are crowdfunding and/or releasing your game in Early Access. I don't have enough of a game to satisfy Early Access so I went the crowdfunding route, I've had some friends have great success with this so I went with this route.
